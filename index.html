Seven Seas Bar and Grill

Nestled in the bustling downtown Ogden Utah, we've been serving delicious carribean food since 2013.

Located at 245 West 25th Street, Ogden, Utah. We would love to have you come by and try some of our food!
Please, take at look at our menu below.

Wine
  • Prosecco
    • A lively Italian sparkler with crisp green apple and honeydew melon flavors. Its light bubbles and clean finish are the perfect palate cleanser.
    • Price: 7$ / 32$
  • Crémant d'Alsace
    • Elegant and refined, offering rich notes of strawberry, pear, and brioche. A creamy texture with a zesty backbone that stands up to richer seafood.
    • Price: 6$ / 30$
  • Rosé de Provence
    • The quintessential dry rosé. Pale pink with delicate aromas of strawberry, citrus zest, and white peach. refreshing and bone-dry.
    • Price: 9$ / 36$
  • Riesling
    • Aromatic and intense, featuring electric acidity balanced by a touch of natural sweetness. Look for notes of lime, apricot, and honeycomb.
    • Price: 5$ / 28$
  • Albariño
    • "Sunshine in a glass." Zesty and saline, with mouth-watering flavors of grapefruit, nectarine, and sea spray. Ideally suited for anything from the ocean.
    • Price: 8$ / 38$
  • Sauvignon Blanc
    • Punchy and herbaceous, exploding with passion fruit, guava, and fresh cut grass. A high-energy wine that matches the vibrancy of Caribbean salsas.
    • Price: 7$ / 32$
  • Viognier
    • Lush and full-bodied, with exotic perfumes of tangerine, peach, and honeysuckle. Its oily texture coats the palate, making it great for creamy sauces.
    • Price: 9$ / 36$
  • Grüner Veltliner
    • A savory and thirst-quenching white with signature notes of white pepper, green apple, and citrus. Versatile and food-friendly.
    • Price: 5$ / 28$

Entrées
  • Jamaican Jerk Chicken
    • Bone-in chicken leg and thigh, slow-smoked over pimento wood and marinated in a fiery blend of scallions, ginger, and scotch bonnet peppers. Served with rice and peas.
    • Price: 24$
  • Curry Goat (Griot Style)
    • Tender chunks of goat meat stewed in a rich, complex curry sauce featuring turmeric, cumin, and thyme. Served with roti bread or steamed rice.
    • Price: 32$
  • Trinidadian Pelau
    • A comforting one-pot dish of chicken, pigeon peas, and rice, colored and flavored by browning the meat in caramelized sugar. Finished with creamy coconut milk.
    • Price: 24$
  • Escovitch Whole Fried Snapper
    • A whole, fresh snapper fried until the skin is perfectly crisp, then topped with a vibrant pickled medley of carrots, onions, and bell peppers (the "Escovitch Sauce").
    • Price: 34$
  • Classic Pepperpot Stew
    • A hearty, slow-simmered stew based on preserved meat (often beef or pork) and indigenous casareep (a caramelized cassava root sauce). Deep, dark, and intensely savory.
    • Price: 29$
  • Seafood Rundown (Run-Down)
    • A light and creamy stew of fresh market fish, shrimp, and vegetables simmered in reduced coconut milk until the sauce thickens and "runs down."
    • Price: 30$
  • Rice and Peas
    • A classic staple of long-grain rice slow-cooked with kidney beans (the "peas"), coconut milk, thyme, and scallions until perfectly fluffy.
    • Price: 8$
  • Steamed White Rice
    • Simple, perfectly steamed long-grain rice, ideal for pairing with rich stews and curries.
    • Price: 6$
  • Fried Plantains (Maduros)
    • Ripe, sweet plantains sliced and fried to achieve a caramelized exterior and soft, sweet interior. Essential for balancing spice.
    • Price: 7$
  • Dahl Puri Roti (Single)
    • A soft, thin flatbread layered with ground split peas, perfect for scooping up curries or wrapping savory fillings
    • Price: 5$
  • Macaroni Pie
    • A creamy, baked casserole similar to mac and cheese, seasoned with mustard and onion, and topped with breadcrumbs for a golden crust. A Trinidadian favorite.
    • Price: 12$
  • Steamed Callaloo
    • Leafy greens (often similar to spinach or taro leaf) gently steamed with okra, coconut milk, and fresh herbs. A flavorful and healthy green side.
    • Price: 9$
